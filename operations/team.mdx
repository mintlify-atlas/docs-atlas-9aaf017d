---
title: "Managing workers, shifts, and timesheets"
description: "Add workers, assign statuses, plan shifts with start and end times, and log timesheet records with hours worked and output metrics per shift."
---

The Team module gives site managers a single place to manage the people on site. You can add workers with their role and department, plan shifts on a weekly schedule, and log timesheet records that capture hours worked and production output per shift. All data is scoped to the active site.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Workers">
    Workers are the core records in the Team module. Each worker belongs to one site and has a status that controls whether they appear in shift planning.

    ## Adding a worker

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Team page">
        Navigate to **Team** in the sidebar. Click **Add Worker** in the top-right corner.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter worker details">
        Fill in the worker form:

        - **Full Name** (required) ‚Äî the worker's legal name as it will appear in reports and timesheets.
        - **Position** ‚Äî job title, e.g. "Drill Operator" or "Site Supervisor".
        - **Department** ‚Äî operational group, e.g. "Extraction" or "Processing".
        - **Hire Date** ‚Äî date the worker joined the site (YYYY-MM-DD).
        - **Status** ‚Äî see the status reference below.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save the record">
        Click **Add Worker**. The worker appears immediately in the Workers table and becomes available for shift assignment.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ## Worker status reference

    | Status | Meaning |
    |--------|---------|
    | `active` | Worker is currently on site and available for shift planning. Only active workers appear in the shift assignment dropdown. |
    | `on_leave` | Worker is temporarily absent (e.g. annual leave, medical). They remain in the roster but are excluded from shift planning. |
    | `terminated` | Worker has left the site permanently. The record is retained for historical timesheet data but is hidden from active workflows. |

    <Tip>
      Update a worker's status rather than deleting the record. Deleting a worker removes their profile, but their past shift records are preserved ‚Äî the worker name will show as "Unknown" in those records.
    </Tip>

    ## Worker field reference

    | Field | Type | Required | Description |
    |-------|------|----------|-------------|
    | `full_name` | text | Yes | Worker's full name |
    | `position` | text | No | Job title or role |
    | `department` | text | No | Operational department |
    | `hire_date` | date | No | Start date on site (YYYY-MM-DD) |
    | `status` | enum | Yes | `active`, `on_leave`, or `terminated` |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Shifts">
    Planned shifts let site managers schedule workers for specific dates and time windows. The weekly schedule view shows all planned shifts within a selected date range.

    ## Planning a shift

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the schedule">
        Go to **Team** and select the **Schedule** tab, or click **Plan Shift** from the Team page header.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select the worker and date">
        Choose an active worker from the dropdown. Workers with `on_leave` or `terminated` status do not appear. Set the **Shift Date** (YYYY-MM-DD).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set the time window">
        Enter **Start Time** and **End Time** in HH:MM format. The system enforces that end time must be after start time.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add optional notes">
        Use the **Notes** field for any special instructions, hazard warnings, or location details relevant to this shift.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save the shift">
        Click **Plan Shift**. The shift appears on the weekly schedule grid for the selected date.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ## Planned shift field reference

    | Field | Type | Required | Description |
    |-------|------|----------|-------------|
    | `worker_id` | uuid | Yes | Links to an active worker record |
    | `shift_date` | date | Yes | The calendar date of the shift (YYYY-MM-DD) |
    | `start_time` | time | Yes | Shift start in HH:MM |
    | `end_time` | time | Yes | Shift end in HH:MM ‚Äî must be after `start_time` |
    | `notes` | text | No | Free-text notes for the shift |

    <Note>
      The weekly schedule view fetches planned shifts between a `from` and `to` date, ordered by date and start time. Navigate weeks using the date picker controls at the top of the schedule.
    </Note>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Timesheets">
    Shift records are the actual timesheet entries ‚Äî logged after a shift is completed. They capture hours worked and an optional production output metric, making them useful for both payroll and productivity analysis.

    ## Logging a timesheet entry

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the log shift form">
        From the **Team** page, click **Log Shift**. You can also click the **Log** button on any worker row to pre-select that worker.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select the worker and date">
        Choose an active worker and enter the **Shift Date**. The date defaults to today.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter hours and output">
        - **Hours Worked** ‚Äî total hours for the shift (0‚Äì24, in 0.5 increments).
        - **Output** ‚Äî the quantity produced during the shift, e.g. `12.5`.
        - **Output Unit** ‚Äî the unit of measurement, e.g. `tonnes` or `carats`.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save the record">
        Click **Log Shift**. The record appears in the **Shift Records** tab, sorted by date descending.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ## Timesheet field reference

    | Field | Type | Required | Description |
    |-------|------|----------|-------------|
    | `worker_id` | uuid | Yes | The worker this record belongs to |
    | `shift_date` | date | Yes | Date the shift was worked (YYYY-MM-DD) |
    | `hours_worked` | numeric | No | Hours on shift (0‚Äì24) |
    | `output_metric` | numeric | No | Quantity produced during the shift |
    | `metric_unit` | text | No | Unit for `output_metric`, e.g. `tonnes`, `carats` |
    | `notes` | text | No | Free-text notes |

    The Team page header shows aggregate stats across all logged shifts for the active site: active worker count, average hours per shift, and total shifts logged. The **Hours by Worker** chart shows cumulative hours across all shift records, up to the top 8 workers by total hours.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
