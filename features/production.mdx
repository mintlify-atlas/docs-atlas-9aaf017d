The Production Log captures the physical output of your mine site on a day-by-day basis. Each entry records ore extracted, waste moved, grade, and water used for a given date, and optionally links the output to a specific customer. Aggregate totals and a cost-per-tonne figure are computed automatically from your logged entries and confirmed expense transactions.

## Field reference

| Field | Type | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `log_date` | date | **Yes** | The date this entry covers (`YYYY-MM-DD`). One entry per site per day. |
| `ore_tonnes` | number | No | Tonnes of ore extracted (`t`) |
| `waste_tonnes` | number | No | Tonnes of waste material moved (`t`) |
| `grade_g_t` | number | No | Ore grade in grams per tonne (`g/t`) |
| `water_m3` | number | No | Volume of water used in cubic metres (`m³`) |
| `notes` | string | No | Free-text field for shift observations or anomalies |

<Note>
  All numeric fields are optional — log only the metrics your site tracks. Blank fields display as `—` in the table and are excluded from averages.
</Note>

## Adding a production log entry

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Log Day">
    Click the **Log Day** button in the top-right corner of the Production Log page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the date">
    The date field defaults to today. Change it if you are back-filling a previous day. If a log already exists for that date on this site, submitting will update the existing record rather than create a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally assign a customer">
    If the output is attributable to a specific customer, select them from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter production metrics">
    Fill in the values you have for this day — ore extracted, waste moved, grade, and water used. Leave any field blank if the data is not available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add notes">
    Use the Notes field to capture shift conditions, equipment issues, or anything relevant to this day's output.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Add Log**. The entry appears at the top of the table (newest first) and the summary cards update immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing and deleting entries

Click any row in the table to re-open the log form pre-populated with that entry's values. Make your changes and click **Update**.

To delete an entry, click the **×** icon on the right side of the row and confirm the deletion. Deletion is permanent.

## Summary cards

Five summary cards at the top of the log page aggregate the data visible in the current filtered view.

| Card | Calculation |
|---|---|
| **Total Ore (t)** | Sum of `ore_tonnes` across all displayed entries |
| **Total Waste (t)** | Sum of `waste_tonnes` across all displayed entries |
| **Avg Grade (g/t)** | Mean of non-null `grade_g_t` values |
| **Days Logged** | Count of log entries in the current filter scope |
| **Cost / Tonne** | Total confirmed site expenses ÷ Total ore extracted |

<Tip>
  Set the customer filter before reading the summary cards when you need to report output and costs for a specific customer contract.
</Tip>

## How production data feeds into reports

Production logs do not create financial transactions on their own. Their value in the reporting layer comes from:

- **Cost per tonne** — the platform divides confirmed `expense` transactions (filtered to the same customer scope) by total ore extracted, giving you an operational efficiency metric alongside financial data.
- **Grade tracking** — average grade across the reporting period can be compared against ore body models and contract targets.
- **Ore-to-waste ratio** — derive the stripping ratio from Total Waste ÷ Total Ore for your operations review.

<Note>
  **Offline behaviour:** Production log entries are queued when your device is offline and replayed on reconnection. If a log for the same `site_id` and `log_date` was created on the server while you were offline, the upsert logic merges records using `site_id, log_date` as the conflict key — the last write wins.
</Note>
